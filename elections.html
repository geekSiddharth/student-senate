Guidelines for elections

When are elections held?

Elections are conducted in April (or the end of March) every year for new members. However, for the incoming batch (BTech, MTech, and PhD), the Student Senate is required to ensure that members are elected, latest, within two months of the commencement of the Academic Year and that every BTech/MTech/PhD batch has adequate representation.
You will receive a proper mail to inform you about he starting of election process.

Positions Available

  • 1 CSE, 1 ECE, 1 CSAM (if applicable), 1 CSD (if applicable), 1 ITSS (if applicable) student each from second, third and fourth year B.Tech.
  • 3 students (any branch) from first year B.Tech
  • 1 M-Tech student each from second year
  • 3 students (one from each branch) from first year M.Tech.
  • 1 PhD student
  • Coordinator, Secretary adn WebAdmin are elected by internal elections
    It is recommended that the positions of Coordinator and Secretary are given to senior students as it involves additional responsibilities, demands on time and interaction with many faculty members/administrative staff which may be easier for students in the elective years

Elections Guidelines

  • The elections will be conducted by the existing Student Senate with Institute representatives overseeing it. The Dean of Student Affairs, however, has the right to declare the election null and void, if he/she feels that there has been some impropriety.
  • The Student Council will nominate an Election Commissioner for each batch, to conduct and oversee the entire process for that batch. An Election Commissioner must be a student of the Institute and cannot be a candidate, and must be neutral and must undertake to be fair and transparent in his/her dealings.
  • The candidates will have to submit their nomination to the Election Commissioner by filling suitable form and providing necessary documentation. The Election Commissioner may disqualify a candidate if he/she does not satisfy the eligibility criteria.
  • Canvassing:
    • Candidates can submit a write up of around 200-250 words to the election commissioner by an announced date, after which the Election Commissioner will ensure that they are all put up on the website
    • The Election Commissioner for a batch may organize interaction/debates at times which do not clash in any way with academics of the Institute
    • As of now, candidates cannot do any form of canvassing, including putting of posters, posting on Social Media. Any candidate, or his/her friends, found engaging in canvassing will be disqualified by the Election Commissioner.
  • The nomination process will have to be completed at least 1 week before the elections. Only full time students who have not served for 2 years, and who satisfy the Institute requirements (e.g, CGPA of 7, unless otherwise stated) for such activities are eligible.
  • In addition, if a student has had a back in more than 1 subject in the winter semester, his/her membership would stand cancelled.
  • A student cannot be a member of the Student Council and Student Senate at the same time. Hence, a student cannot contest for both the elections, if they are happening together.
  • In event of a Student Senate member changing his/her stream after getting elected to represent his/her stream, the said Student Senate member will have to forgo his membership in the Senate. After this, fresh elections will be conducted to fill this vacant seat, ensuring a proportionate representation of the stream in the Senate.
  • The elections will be conducted in the lunch time on a specific day so that it does not disrupt the normal flow of academic affairs. The Election Commissioner for each batch will be responsible for conducting this, and may seek help from the Dean of Student Affairs to ensure proper conduct.
  • Only students from that particular batch whose elections are being conducted will be allowed to vote. For example, if student representatives of 2nd year are being selected then only 2nd year students will be allowed to vote.
  • The counting of votes will be done by the Election Commissioner under the supervision of Dean of Student Affairs. All ballots will be preserved, and in case of a close contest (a margin of 5% or less), a recounting will be done. A candidate can also request recounting.
  • The main objective is to ensure that the elections are held in a fair manner and there are no cases of vote forgery, extensive campaigning, etc.
